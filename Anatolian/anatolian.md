## A

### <a id="-a">\*-a, -ā 'allative singular ending' (n)</a>
The allative answers the question ‘to where’ and is a living case in the OH and MH period only. Its ending is either -a or -ā. The difference between the two is clearly a matter of accentuation, compare a-aš-ka /ʔā́ska/ ‘gate’, ha-me-eš-ha-an-da /HmésHanta/, lu-li-ya /lū́lia/ ‘pond’, ne-e-pí-ša /nébisa/ ‘heaven’, šu-uh-ha /sóHa/ ‘roof’ vs. iš-ša-a /ɨSā́/ ‘mouth’, ki-iš-ra-a /kɨSrā́/ ‘hand’, ta-ak-na-a /tgnā́/ ‘earth’ (all OS attestations). On the basis of pa-ra-a /prā́/, an original allative of the paradigm to which peran and parza belong as well, which can be directly compared to Gr. πρό, Skt. prá-, Lat. prŏ- and Goth. fra- < *pró, I assume that the    allative ending has to be reconstructed as *-o. \
