# Hittite

## A

### <a id="-a">\*-a, -ā "allative singular ending" (n)</a>
The allative answers the question ‘to where’ and is a living case in the OH and MH period only. Its ending is either _-a_ or _-ā_. The difference between the two is clearly a matter of accentuation, compare _a-as-ka_ /ʔā́ska/ ‘gate’, _ha-me-es-ha-an-da_ /HmésHanta/, _lu-li-ya_ /lū́lia/ ‘pond’, _ne-e-pí-sa_ /nébisa/ ‘heaven’, _su-uh-ha_ /sóHa/ ‘roof’ vs. _is-sa-a_ /ɨSā́/ ‘mouth’, _ki-is-ra-a_ /kɨSrā́/ ‘hand’, _ta-ak-na-a_ /tgnā́/ ‘earth’ (all OS attestations). On the basis of _pa-ra-a_ /prā́/, an original allative of the paradigm to which peran and parza belong as well, which can be directly compared to Gr. _πρό_, Skt. _prá-_, Lat. _prŏ-_ and Goth. _fra_- < _*pró_, I assume that the    allative ending has to be reconstructed as \*-o. \
