\lex -eh₂ -oh₂ (1)
\class suff
\gloss _allative suffix_
\notes Anatolian shows a clear alternation between a-colour and o-colour in this suffix, indicating that the ending cannot be simple \*-o as found in many PIE directional adverbs.
\ref melchert2017a

\lex -eh₂- (3)
\class ey v.suff
\gloss _denominal factitive_
\notes The h₂e-inflection is based on the Hittite hi-inflection, and the strong tendency in Core IE to thematise the suffix (cf. the word equation Hittite _newahhi_ = Latin _novō_ = Greek _neáō_).
\ref EDHIL:164

\lex h₁óh₁s
\stem h₁(e)h₁s-
\class n n
\gloss mouth
\notes Core IE attests to PIE \*HóHs/\*HǻHs, while Anatolian refutes the presence of \*h₃. Only Hittite reflects the original oblique - Core IE has levelled the nominative-accusative form throughout the paradigm and derivatives.
