### <a id="abalo">\*abalo- "apple" (n)</a>
GOID: OIr. _ubull_ [o n]
W: MW _afal_ [m]
BRET: OBret. _abal_, MBret. _aval_
GAUL: _auallo_ gl. _poma_
PIE: \*hβ²²ephβ²Éol- "apple"
COGN: OHG _apful_, OCS _abl―äko_, Lith. _Ο≥buolas_, Osc. _Abella_ (Toponym)
SEE: [\*abalnΡ¹ "apple-tree"](#abalna)
ETYM: The geminate -_ll_- in OIr. could have arisen under analogical influence of \*abalnΡ¹ "apple-tree". The Celtic forms can be derived from the oblique cases of the PIE l-stem, with the nom.sg. \*hβ²²ephβ²Éol (or \*hβ²²ephβ²É≈çl) / gen.sg. \*hβ²²(e)phβ²Élos. It is assumed here that \*hβ²É voiced the preceding \*p (as in \*piphβ²Éeti "drinks" > Skt. _pΟ≠bati_), but there is no independent evidence for a laryngeal in the word for "apple", so the alternative PIE reconstruction \*hβ²²ebol- (with the rare phoneme \*b) is also possible.

### <a id="abon">\*abon- "river" (n)</a>
GOID: OIr. _ab, aub_ [n f]
W: MW _afon_ [f]
BRET: MBret. _auon_
CO: Co. _auon_
GAUL: _ambe_ gl. _riuo_
PIE: \*hβ²²ep-hβ²Éon- "river"
COGN: Hitt. _hΡ¹pa_-, Pal. _hΡ¹pna_-, Lat. _amnis_
ETYM: OIr. _aub_ clearly points to PCelt. \*abu, acc. \*abonen; the form \*abonΡ¹ (> MW _afori_) was built to this stem. The n-stem is probably inherited from PIE (cf. the Pal. and Lat. reflexes). Gaul. _ambe_, with -_m_-, may be corrupted, but cf. the hydronyms _Amyr_ (MW), _Ammer_ (Germ.), _Ambre_ (Fr.), which are probably Celtic (they may represent \*ambr(iy)o-, but they are also often derived from PIE \*nb Α-ro-, cf. Lat. _imber_ "rain"). A probable derivative from PCelt. \*abon- is PCelt. \*abanko- "beaver", cf. MIr. _abacc_ [o m] "dwarf", perhaps also "beaver", MW _afanc_ [m] "beaver, dwarf", MoBret. _avank_ [m] "dwarf, sea monster". The PIE root is reconstructed as \*hβ²²ep- with the (?possessive) suffix \*-hβ²Éon-, under the assumption that \*phβ²É > \*b (as in \*piphβ²Éeti "drinks" > Skt. pΟ≠bati). If this is correct, the words adduced here are etymologically related to Skt. _ΟΓp_- "water", OPr. _ape_ "brook", etc.

### <a id="ad">\*ad "to" (prp)</a>
GOID: OIr. _ad_-
W: OW _ad_, W _add_-
GAUL: _Ad-ianto_- (PN)
PIE: \*hβ²²ed "to, at"
COGN: Lat. _ad_, Go. _at_-
ETYM: MW _eddyl_ "target" is presumably derived from PCelt. \*adilo-, with the same suffix as OE _til_ "to, until", cf. also OE _tilian_ "reach". In principle, \*adilo can be an adjectival form built to the loc. sg. \*ad-i- (Michiel de Vaan, p. c.). If so, the Germanic forms are from \*hβ²²dilo-, which means that the PIE root had ablaut (\*hβ²²ed- / \*hβ²²d-). This supports the derivation of PCelt. \*dΡΪ "from" from the same root.
