## Α

### <a id="ἁ-"> ἁ- “copulative prefix (α ἀθροιστικόν)” (pref)</a>
VAR: By Grassmann’s dissimilation or by psilosis also ἀ-, which was analogically extended: [ἄλοχος](#ἄλοχος), [ἀδελφός](#ἀδελφός); [ἄπεδος](#ἄπεδος) “even”, [ἄβιος](#ἄβιος) “rich”. A form like Hom. [ἄκτοιτις](#ἄκτοιτις) was not aspirated because the Attic redactors of the text did not know the word, so they followed the Ionic pronunciation (cf. [ἤλιος](#ἤλιος) next to [ἠέλιος](#ἠέλιος)). \
PIE: \*sm̥- \
COGN: Skt. _sa-_ (_sá-nāman-_ “with the same name”), Lat. _sem-_, _sim-_ (_sim-plex_) \
See also [εἷς](#εἷς), [ὀμός](#ὀμός), [ἄμα](#ἄμα). From the meaning “together, provided with”, the so-called α ἐπιτατικόν (intensive) developed, e.g. ἄεδνον [...] πολύφερνον “with much dowry” (H.), cf. [ἕδνα](#ἕδνα). It has been supposed that in some cases a comparable ἀ- arose from \*n̥- (the zero grade of \*h₁en “in”), e.g. [ἀλέγω](#ἀλέγω) (Seiler KZ 75 (1957): 1-23), but the alleged instances are probably all wrong. \
REF: [EDG]\: 1

### <a id="ἀ-1"> ἀ- “privative prefix (α στερητικόν)” (pref)</a>
VAR: Prevocalic ἀν-. \
PIE: \*n̥- \
COGN: Skt. _a(n)-_, Lat. _in-_, Go. _un-_ \
The antevocalic form ἀν- arose because of a following laryngeal (Indo-European roots always started with a consonant), which led to vocalization of the nasal: \*n̥-HV- > \*anV-. In Greek, the loss of initial consonants (\*u̯-, \*s-) disturbed the original distribution: thus [ἄισος](#ἄισος); (< \*ἀ-ϝισος) next to the  reshaped [ἄνισος](#ἄνισος). Sometimes this led to analogical forms, like [ἄοζος](#ἄοζος) next to original [ἄνοζος](#ἄνοζος). In the Myc. PN _a-u-po-no_ /Ahupnos/, the _a-_ stands before _h-_, like in classical [ἄυπνος](#ἄυπνος). In [ἀόριστος](#ἀόριστος) (to [ὅρος](#ὅρος) < \*worwo-), we see that the initial ϝ- was originally retained before \*ο as well. \
If the second member began with laryngeal + cons., this yielded Greek νη-, νᾱ-, νω- like in [νήγρετος](#νήγρετος), [νωδός](#νωδός) < \*n-h₁gr-, \*n-h₃d-. These adjectives were later reshaped, e.g. into [ἀνώνυμος](#ἀνώνυμος) (see Beekes 1969: 98-113). In [ἀνάεδος](#ἀνάεδος), [ἀνάελπτος](#ἀνάελπτος) and [ἀνάπνευστος](#ἀνάπνευστος), which seem to show ἀνα- “un-”, the last two are analogical, and the first may stand for ἀν-εεδν-. On pleonastic ἀ(ν)-, cf. [ἀβέλτερος](#ἀβέλτερος). \
ἀ(ν)- was originally limited to verbal adjectives and bahuvrīhis, both in Greek and in other IE  languages; see Frisk 1941: 4ff., 44ff., Frisk 1948: 8ff., Wackernagel 1920-1924(2): 284ff.,  Wackernagel 1920-1924(1): 282f., and Moorhouse 1959. The sentence negative was \*ne, in  Lat. _ne-scio_, _ne-fas_, etc., but this use is not attested for Greek (not in [νέποδες](#νέποδες)). \
REF: [EDG]\: 1

### <a id="ἀ-2">ἀ- (pref)</a>
In Pre-Greek substrate words, a "prothetic vowel" occurs, e.g. [ἄσταχυς](#ἄσταχυς) next to [στάχυς](#στάχυς). In other words, the prothetic vowel may be present in  the reflex of a substrate word or not. \
Without a doubt, a phonetic process was at the origin of these variations. It is rather frequent, see Fur.: 368-378. The vowel was almost always ἀ- (only very few exceptions can be recorded). \
REF: [EDG]\: 1-2

### <a id="ἀ-3">ἀ- (pref)</a>
Found in Anatolian place names, e.g. Ἀπαισός / Παισός, Ἄθυμβρα / Θύμβρα. \
The origin of the phenomenon is unknown, and could be different from that of the Pre-Greek prothetic vowel. It is probably due to adaptation from a non-IE language. \
REF: [EDG]\: 2

### <a id="ἀάατος">ἀάατος (adj?)</a>
VAR: For varying length of the vowel, see _LfgrE_ s.v. \
Meaning unknown. In νῡν ὄμοσσον ἀάατος Στυγὸς ὕδωρ [Ξ 271] “inviolable”?, ἄεθλος ἀάατος [φ 91, χ 5] “infallible”?, κάρτος ἀάατος [A. R. 2, 77] “invisible”? \
Comparable to [ἄτη](#ἄτη) and [ἀάω](#ἀάω) “to damage”. Cf. perhaps ἀάβακτοι [...] ἀβλαβεῖς “undamaged” [H.]. Note that the privative prefix is ἀ-, not ἀν-. \
REF: [EDG]\: 2

### <a id="ἄαδα">ἄαδα “want, lack (Lacon.)” [H. ἔνδεια. Λάκωνες]</a>
DER: ἀαδεῖν· ἀπορεῖσθαι, ἀσιτεῖν “to be in distress, abstain from food” [H.], so on [ἄδην](#ἄδην). We also find ἀαδεῖν· ὀχλεῖν, λυμεῖσθαι, ἀδικεῖν “to disturb, be vexed, be wronged” (s.v. [ἀνδάνω](#ἀνδάνω), [ἡδύς](#ἡδύς)). \
The forms and meanings are uncertain; see DELG for an uncertain suggestion by Frisk. Latte assumes ἄδδ = ἄζα, contratry to the alphabetical order. \
REF: [EDG]\: 2

### <a id="ἀάω">ἀάω “to damage”, med. “act in blindness” [Il.] (v)</a>
VAR: Besides pres. ἀᾶται [T 91 = 129] < \*ἀϝάεται onl aor. ἄασα (contracted ἆσα) < \*ἄϝᾰσα, med. -άμην, pass. ἀάσθην. With -σκ-: ἀάσκει· φθείρει, βλάπτει “destroys, damages” [H.]; difficult κατέβασκε· κατέβλαψεν “damaged” [H.], for \*κατ-αβασκε? \
DER: [ἀεσίφρων](#ἀεσίφρων) (wrong for ἀασι-) “damaged in mind” [Il.], cf. ἀασιφόρος· βλάβην φέρων “bringing damage” [H.]; [ἀνατ(ε)ί](#ἀνατεί) “without harm, with impunity” [A.]. \
PIE: \*h₂euh₂-? \
For PGr. \*awa-je/o-, we may mechanically reconstruct an IE root \*h₂euh₂-, but there are no known cognates. A verbal noun \*awa-teh₂-, with which compare [αὐάτα](#αὐάτα) [Alc.], yields [ἄτη](#ἄτη) “damage, guilt, delusion”. Not connected to [ὠτειλή](#ὠτειλή). \
REF: [EDG]\: 3

### <a id="ἄβα">ἄβα “wheel or screaming” [H. τροχὸς ἢ βοή] (v)</a>
In the sense of “screaming”, Specht compared ἄβα with Hom. [αὖε](#αὔε) [ipf.] “called”. See further the glosses ἀβήρει· ᾄδει “sings” and ἀβέσσει· ἐπιποθεῖ, θορυβεῖ “yearns for, makes noise” [both H.]; lastly also [ἄβωρ](#ἄβωρ) with the meaning [βοή](#βοή). See [αὐδή](#αὐδή), [ἀείδω](#ἀείδω). \
REF: [EDG]\: 3
